Structure of the experiment

  • You will face 10 rounds in this experiment.
  • Each round is independent of other rounds.
  • Each round is equally likely to be selected for your payment.
  • You might be matched with different group members in each round.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each round seriously.

Structure of the round

Stage 1: Information

There is an urn called the “Unknown Urn” that is filled with multiple balls with different colors. The urn is either the ``RED'' urn, including two small red urns: RED Urn (1) and RED Urn (2), or the ``BLUE'' urn, including two small blue urns: BLUE Urn (1) and BLUE Urn (2). As you can see in the following Figure 1, the ``RED'' urn has more red balls, and the ``BLUE'' urn has more blue balls.




  • At the beginning of each round, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) to determine which urn to use - either the ``RED'' urn or ``BLUE'' urn. But you won’t know which one is chosen.
  • Every 3 participants in this lab are randomly selected into one group.
  • After the group is formed, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) again to determine which small urn to use - either the Urn (1) or Urn (2). Each of you can randomly pick up one ball from the chosen small urn.
  • IMPORTANT! You will be labeled as ‘‘RED’’ if you have picked a red ball. You will be labeled as ‘‘BLUE’’ if you have picked a blue ball.
  • Your label (not the small Urn!) will be shown to other two participants in your group. You will also be informed about the label (not the small Urn!) of other participants. For example, if you picked a red ball from Urn (1), you will be shown the following screenshot:




Stage 2: Who you want to communicate with

Following by Stage 1, you have a chance to gain more information by communicating with other participants. BUT you can only communicate with no more than people in your group. ALSO even you want to communicate with other participants, they must agree with communication with you, i.e. communication is bilateral.

  • Your Task: you need to rank from who you most want to communicate with to who you least want to communicate with. You will see the following screenshot:




  • All participants need to rank the choices. IF you have no strict preference between two choices, you can always put them in the same box.
  • How you match with other participants: the computer will match you with others based on your ranking, i.e. the computer will match you first with the group member you put in the first box, then with the one in the second box, and so on until the last box.
  • So it is IMPORTANT for you to rank exactly based on who you want to communicate with.
  • Once all group members make their choices, you will enter the next page. If you are not successfully connected with other participants, you just wait in the page for two minutes. If you are successfully connected with another participant, you can (I) see he/she picked a ball from which small urn: either Urn (1) or Urn (2); (II) freely to talk with him/her for 2 minutes. You will see the following screenshot:




Stage 3: Voting

  • Now you need to cast a vote about which Urn is chosen: RED or BLUE.
  • Your payoff is determined by the number of correct votes: If there are 2 or 3 participants in your group vote correctly, each of you in the group will earn £15！ If there are 0 or 1 participant in your group vote correctly, each of you in the group will only earn £2!
