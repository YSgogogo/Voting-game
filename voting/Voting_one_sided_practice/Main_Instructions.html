Structure of the experiment

  • You will face 15 rounds in today's experiment.
  • Each round is independent of other rounds.
  • Each round is equally likely to be selected for your earnings.
  • You might be matched with different participants as group members in each round.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each round seriously.

Structure of the round

Stage 1: Information

There is a state called ''unknown state'' that is filled with multiple balls with different colors in two boxes. The state is either the ''RED'', which includes two boxes: Box A and Box B, or the ''BLUE'', which includes two boxes: Box A and Box B. As you can see in the following Figure, the ''RED'' has more red balls, and the ''BLUE'' has more blue balls in each box.




  • At the beginning of each round, every 5 participants in this lab will be randomly selected into one group, and each participant will be given a unique ID for that round.
  • In your group, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) to determine the “unknown state” - either ''RED'' or ''BLUE''. No one will be told which one is actually selected!
  • For each of you, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) again to determine a box from the selected state - either Box A or Box B.
  • For each of you, the computer will randomly and independently pick a ball from your selected box - either or .
  • The ball that each group member got will be shown to other group members, BUT the selected box will not be shown to other group members (your box information is private).
  • After knowing these information, you are asked to choose whom you want to send your private information (i.e., your box information) to:
    • a)   send to a player who got
    b)   send to a player who got
    c)   do not send to anyone

    N.B. 1. If there is more than one group member (except you) who got the same ball, the computer will randomly send your private information to one of them; 2. if no group member (except you) got the red ball, option a) will not be available; if no group member (except you) got the blue ball, option b) will not be available.

Stage 2: Voting

  • Once all group members have finished Stage 1, you will be shown the following:
    • 1)   the left table will have more box information if other group members choose to send their private box information to you.
    2)   the network diagram in the middle includes the flow of information.
    3)   you need to vote for which state was chosen at the beginning: RED or BLUE.



  • Your earnings: the computer will randomly determine a group member, if he/she votes correctly, each of you in the group will earn £15！ If he/she votes incorrectly, each of you in the group will only earn £2！
